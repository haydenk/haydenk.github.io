### Relational Emphasis  
1. Human flourishing & discipleship are impossible in isolation; devices often mask functional loneliness.  
2. Men especially default to “lone-ranger” living; genuine growth requires “corner-turers” (Mark 2 paralytic image).  
3. Four essential relationship categories (modeled from David):
   * Joab: peer who shares mission/drive.
   * Nathan: older voice who can rebuke.
   * Jonathan: covenant friend.
   * Timothy: younger protégé you invest in.  
4. Smartphones (2007) + pulpit silence on cultural issues drove many to podcasts for worldview formation. Nature abhors a vacuum; podcasters became prophets, therapists became secular priests.  

### Timothy Case Study

* Mixed heritage: Jewish mother (Eunice), Greek father.
* Prominent in Acts; co-listed with Paul on six NT letters (2 Cor, Phil, Col, 1-2 Thess, Philem).
* Uncircumcised until Acts 16; Paul circumcised him to remove gospel obstacles.
* Struggled with fear/timidity; Paul repeatedly affirmed him.
* Eventually senior pastor at Ephesus, the NT's largest church; plagued by stomach issues.
* Take-away: Every believer needs a Paul and owes the Kingdom a Timothy.

## Theological / Exegetical Points  
1. Wisdom is “more valuable than wealth or health” (Pro 4:7); first step is aggressively seeking it.  
2. Lord’s Prayer uses plural pronouns, underscoring assumed community.  
3. “Show me your group chat and I’ll show you your future” — application of Proverbs 13:20 principle.  
4. Pastoral caution: if podcast hosts contradict your local pastor on moral/theological matters, default to the shepherd who actually knows you.

## Practical Applications  
1. Identify four “corner-turers” who will carry you to Jesus when you’re spiritually lame.  
2. Proactively pursue wisdom: locate a believer who excels in an area you lack (marriage, parenting, finance) and “buy them lunch, ask a million questions, then do what they say.”  
3. Join Rooted (10-week discipleship experience). Text “ROOTED” to 20411.  
4. Vet all influencers: Road, Trail, or Minefield? If it’s a minefield (e.g., Fuentes), find the same good insight elsewhere.  
5. Use the podcast’s downloadable discipleship guide for a 1-hour weekly meet-up (coffee, sermon recap, confession, prayer).  
6. Fathers: replace screens with shared experiences; sons learn life skills from present dads, not algorithms.
